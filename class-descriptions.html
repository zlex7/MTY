Class Descriptions

If you have any questions about the courses offered at MTY Academy, please visit the FAQ page or contact us here.

Summer 05 Pre-Algebra

This class is an entry level class at MTY Academy, but it is recommended that the student be a rising 5th grader.

Topics covered in this class include:
• Number lines and Negative Numbers
• Arithmetic Operations on Integers (Focusing on Negative Integers)
• Divisibility Rules for Integers Up to 10
• Reducing Fractions, Improper Fractions, and Mixed Numbers
• Linear Equations in One Variable
• Applications of Linear Equations
• The Four-Step Procedure of Solving Word Problems

Fall 05 Pre-Algebra

This is the first semester of the yearly course, 05 Pre-Algebra. Its prerequisite is the Summer 05 Pre-Algebra course.

Topics covered in this class include:
• The Greatest Common Factor (GCF) of Two Integers
• The Least Common Multiple (LCM) of Two Integers
• Three Methods for Finding the GCF and LCM
• Linear Inequalities
• Systems of Linear Inequalities
• Applications of Linear Equations and Inequalities

Spring 05 Pre-Algebra

This is the second semester of the yearly course, 05 Pre-Algebra. Its prerequisite is the Fall 05 Pre-Algebra course.

Topics covered in this class include:
• Percentages and Their Applications
• Ratios and Proportions and Their Applications
• The Cartesian Coordinate Plane
• Distance Between Two Points
• The Midpoint of a Line Segment
• Applications to Triangles on a Plane

Summer 06 Geometry

This is the first Geometry course offered by MTY Academy. Its prerequisite is the Spring 05 Algebra course.

Topics covered in this class include:
• Line Segments, Parallel and Perpendicular Lines, and Transversals
• Line Segment Bisectors and Angle Bisectors
• Angles (Vertical, Corresponding, Alternate Interior/Exterior, Complementary, Supplementary)
• Triangles and Their Properties
• Three Methods for Finding the Area of a Triangle
• Special Triangles (Isosceles, Equilateral, Right)
• Polygons and their Properties
• Circles and Their Properties

Math Exploration

This course is designed for rising 6th to 8th graders, and it is suggested but not required for the student to have taken the 05 Algebra courses. This course is split into two parts: Sequences and Combinatorics.

Topics covered in this class include:
• Arithmetic and Geometric Sequences
• Summation of Finite Terms and Special Formulas
• Basic Combinatorics
• Sum Rule, Product Rule, and Principle of Inclusion-Exclusion
• Permutations and Combinations With and Without Repitition
• Binomial Theory

Summer 05 English

This course is designed for rising 5th graders, and is writing intensive. The purpose of this course is to develop and improve English skills and help them further develop these skills through independent novel reading, discussion, analysis, comprehension, vocabulary, and both expository and personal narrative essay writing. Essay structure and format will be further developed in this course, as will the practices of organization and note-taking. Students will be reading Heroes, Gods, and Monsters of the Greek Myths by Bernard Evslin.

Fall 05 English

This English course is for the 5th grade student, and is a continuation of our 5th grade English curriculum. Its prerequisite is Summer 05 English. The purpose of this course is to further refine students' general English skills through independent reading, analysis, comprehension, vocabulary, summary, main idea, and essay writing skills. Writing in the correct format will be practiced through summaries, various warm up activities, and essays. Students will be reading Gary Paulsen's novel Hatchet.
